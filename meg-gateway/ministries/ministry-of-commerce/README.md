## Role & Jurisdiction

The Ministry of Commerce oversees all commercial activity in Saudi Arabia, developing policies related to trade and the business sector. It is the primary regulatory body for:

- **Company registration and commercial registration (السجل التجاري)**
- **Commercial licensing**
- **Consumer protection**
- **Foreign trade regulation**
- **Corporate governance and compliance**

### Key Subsidiary Agencies
- Deputy Ministry for Consumer Protection
- Deputy Ministry for Industry Affairs
- Deputy Ministry for Foreign Trade
- Deputy Ministry of Internal Trade
- Deputy Ministry for Technical Affairs
- General Authority for Foreign Trade (child agency)

## Foreign Investment Regulations

### Company Registration for Foreign Entities

Foreign companies wishing to operate in Saudi Arabia must:

1. **Obtain an Investment License from MISA** (Ministry of Investment) — this is the mandatory first step before any commercial registration
2. **Register with the Ministry of Commerce** — obtain a Commercial Registration (CR / سجل تجاري) through the ministry's electronic services
3. **Comply with the Companies Law** — Royal Decree M/132 (Companies Law), which governs all company formations in the Kingdom

### Legal Entity Types Available to Foreign Investors

Under the Saudi Companies Law, foreign investors may establish:

| Entity Type | Arabic | Key Features |
|-------------|--------|--------------|
| **Limited Liability Company (LLC)** | شركة ذات مسؤولية محدودة | Most common for foreign investors. Min 1 partner, no minimum capital requirement (as of 2023 Companies Law amendments) |
| **Joint Stock Company (JSC)** | شركة مساهمة | For larger enterprises. Can be public or private. Min capital SAR 500,000 for private |
| **Branch Office** | فرع شركة أجنبية | Extension of a foreign parent company |
| **Professional Company** | شركة مهنية | For licensed professionals |
| **Simplified Joint Stock Company** | شركة مساهمة مبسطة | New form introduced in 2023 Companies Law, designed for startups and SMEs |

### Commercial Registration Requirements

To obtain a Commercial Registration (CR), a foreign entity must provide:

1. Valid MISA investment license
2. Articles of Association (authenticated and translated to Arabic)
3. Board resolution or partner agreement
4. Lease agreement for a physical office in Saudi Arabia
5. Capital deposit confirmation (via Saudi bank)
6. National Address registration (via Saudi Post / العنوان الوطني)

### Electronic Services

The Ministry processes registrations through:
- **Online portal:** https://mc.gov.sa/en/eservices/Pages/default.aspx
- **Financial statements platform (Qawaem):** https://qawaem.bc.gov.sa/en/Pages/default.aspx
- **Mobile apps available** via mc.gov.sa

## Licensing Requirements

### Commercial License Categories

1. **Trade License** — for import/export and wholesale/retail
2. **Industrial License** — for manufacturing (coordinated with Ministry of Industry)
3. **Service License** — for professional and consulting services
4. **Contracting License** — for construction and project-based work

### Key Compliance Requirements

- **Financial Statements:** All companies must prepare and submit annual financial statements within 6 months of fiscal year end (Article 17, Companies Law). Submission via Qawaem platform. Penalties apply for non-compliance (Article 262, Companies Law).
- **Accounting Standards:** Must follow accounting standards adopted in the Kingdom (IFRS as endorsed by SOCPA)
- **Annual Renewal:** Commercial registrations must be renewed annually
- **Saudization (Nitaqat):** Companies must meet minimum Saudi employee quotas as per Ministry of Human Resources regulations

## Recent Changes (2025–2026)

### Companies Law Amendments (effective 2023, fully implemented through 2025)
- Removal of minimum capital requirements for LLCs
- Introduction of Simplified Joint Stock Company (SJSc) structure
- Enhanced corporate governance requirements
- Stricter penalties for non-compliance with financial reporting (Articles 262+)
- Companies penalized if financial statements not submitted by deadline (enforcement active as of June 2025)

### Digital Transformation
- Continued expansion of fully digital commercial registration processes
- Integration with MISA's digital investment license system
- National Address requirement enforced electronically

### Investment Law Changes
- The new Investment Law (Royal Decree, approved 2024, implementation 2025-2026) significantly expands foreign investor protections and simplifies processes — see Ministry of Investment file for details

## Important Notes for Foreign Investors

1. **MISA license is prerequisite** — no commercial registration without investment license
2. **Negative List** — some activities remain restricted or prohibited for foreign investors (MISA maintains the list)
3. **GCC Nationals** have preferential treatment compared to other foreign nationals
4. **Arabic is the official language** for all legal documents and contracts with government
5. **Saudization quotas** apply to all companies and are sector-specific
