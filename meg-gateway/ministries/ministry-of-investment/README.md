## Role & Jurisdiction

MISA is the **primary government body responsible for foreign investment** in Saudi Arabia. It:

- Issues investment licenses to foreign investors
- Develops the national investment environment
- Manages the National Investment Strategy
- Negotiates international investment agreements
- Provides investor support services
- Manages the Invest Saudi platform (investsaudi.sa)

### Key Programs & Platforms
- **Invest Saudi** — 15 priority sectors with investment opportunities (https://investsaudi.sa)
- **Regional Headquarters (RHQ) Program** — incentivizes MNCs to base regional HQs in Saudi Arabia
- **Visiting Investor Visa** — streamlined visa for prospective investors
- **Tayseer Program** — launched 2017 to improve investment climate

## Foreign Investment Regulations

### Investment License (الترخيص الاستثماري)

**All foreign investors must obtain a MISA investment license before operating in Saudi Arabia.** This is the foundational regulatory requirement.

#### License Application Process

1. **Apply through MISA's e-services portal** (online application)
2. **Submit required documents:**
   - Company incorporation documents (from home country)
   - Board resolution authorizing Saudi investment
   - Financial statements (last 3 years)
   - Business plan for Saudi operations
   - Passport copies of authorized representatives
3. **MISA reviews and approves** — typical processing time varies by sector and complexity
4. **License issued** — enables commercial registration with Ministry of Commerce

### Foreign Ownership Rules

#### Current Framework (as of 2025-2026)

| Aspect | Rule |
|--------|------|
| **100% Foreign Ownership** | Permitted in most sectors (significant expansion since 2017) |
| **Negative List** | MISA maintains a list of activities restricted or prohibited for foreign investment |
| **Engineering Sector** | 100% foreign ownership allowed since August 2017 (initially limited to established MNCs) |
| **Pharmaceutical** | 100% foreign ownership granted (Pfizer precedent, 2016) |
| **Retail & Wholesale** | Generally open to foreign investment |
| **Professional Services** | Open with specific licensing requirements |

#### New Investment Law (نظام الاستثمار الجديد)

The updated Investment Law, approved in 2024 with implementation through 2025-2026, represents the most significant reform to Saudi investment regulation:

**Key Provisions:**
- **Equal treatment** principle — foreign and domestic investors receive same protections
- **Removal of minimum capital** requirements for most investment categories
- **Simplified licensing** — streamlined procedures, shorter processing times
- **Dispute resolution** — enhanced mechanisms including international arbitration
- **Investor protections** — against arbitrary expropriation, with fair compensation guarantees
- **Tax incentives** — aligned with sector-specific programs
- **Transferability** — guaranteed ability to repatriate profits and capital

### Restricted & Prohibited Activities (Negative List)

MISA maintains a negative list of activities closed or restricted for foreign investors. Key sectors historically restricted include:

- Oil exploration and production (reserved for Saudi Aramco/state)
- Military and defense industries (some exceptions for JVs)
- Certain real estate activities in Makkah and Madinah
- Some security services
- Specific recruitment services

*Note: The negative list is regularly updated and has been significantly reduced in recent years as part of Vision 2030 liberalization.*

## Investment Incentives

### National Investment Strategy Incentives

| Incentive | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Corporate Tax** | 20% for foreign entities (standard rate) |
| **Special Economic Zones** | Reduced tax rates, customs exemptions |
| **RHQ Program** | Tax holidays and incentives for regional headquarters |
| **Sector-specific incentives** | Vary by priority sector — available through Invest Saudi |
| **Land allocation** | Available through industrial cities (MODON) |
| **Financing** | Access to Saudi development funds |

## Visa & Sponsorship for Foreign Investors

### Visiting Investor Visa
- New visa category for prospective investors exploring opportunities
- Applied for through Invest Saudi platform
- Streamlined process, does not require a sponsor

### Business/Work Visa Requirements
- After investment license obtained, company can sponsor employees
- Coordinated with Ministry of Human Resources and Ministry of Interior
- Premium Residency visa available (المقيم المتميز) for high-value investors

## Recent Changes (2025–2026)

### Ministerial Change (February 2026)
- **Fahd bin Abduljalil Al-Saif** appointed as Minister of Investment, replacing Khalid Al-Falih
- Al-Saif previously led PIF's investment strategy; brings institutional investing perspective

## Important Notes for Foreign Investors

1. **MISA license is the FIRST step** — all other registrations follow from this
2. **Invest Saudi is the official platform** for exploring opportunities
3. **Visiting Investor Visa** can be obtained before committing to investment
4. **RHQ requirement** — by 2024+, companies wanting Saudi government contracts must have RHQ in the Kingdom
5. **New minister signals continuity** — Al-Saif's PIF background signals focus on institutional and strategic investment
