## Role & Jurisdiction

### MCIT (Ministry)
The Ministry sets policy and strategy for the ICT sector. It is responsible for:
- Developing national ICT policies and strategies
- Digital transformation initiatives
- Advancing the digital economy
- International ICT cooperation
- Supervising the sector's development under Vision 2030

### CST (Regulator)
The Communications, Space & Technology Commission (formerly CITC — Communications and Information Technology Commission) is the **independent regulatory authority** that:
- Issues telecom and technology licenses
- Regulates telecom operators and service providers
- Manages spectrum allocation
- Oversees space technology regulation
- Enforces quality of service standards
- Regulates internet services and content
- Manages domain name registration (.sa)

## Foreign Investment in the Telecom Sector

### Overview

The Saudi telecom sector has been progressively liberalized since the early 2000s. Foreign investment is **permitted** in the telecom sector, subject to specific licensing from CST and investment license from MISA.

### Licensing Framework

#### Telecom Operator Licenses

| License Type | Description | Foreign Ownership |
|-------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Unified License** | Full-service telecom (fixed + mobile + data) | Permitted with conditions |
| **Mobile Virtual Network Operator (MVNO)** | Virtual mobile services | Permitted |
| **Internet Service Provider (ISP)** | Data and internet services | Permitted |
| **Value-Added Services** | Content, applications, and value-added telecom services | Generally open |
| **Satellite Services** | Satellite communication services | Subject to security review |

#### Key Telecom Operators in Saudi Arabia
- **Saudi Telecom Company (STC)** — الاتصالات السعودية — majority state-owned, publicly listed
- **Mobily (Etihad Etisalat)** — موبايلي — partially foreign-owned (Etisalat Group)
- **Zain Saudi Arabia** — زين — subsidiary of Zain Group (Kuwait)

*These existing operators demonstrate that significant foreign participation in the Saudi telecom market is already established.*

### Requirements for Foreign Telecom Companies

1. **MISA Investment License** — mandatory first step
2. **CST Telecom License** — specific to the type of service
3. **Technical Requirements:**
   - Network infrastructure plans
   - Quality of service commitments
   - Coverage obligations (for mobile operators)
   - Emergency service obligations
4. **Security Requirements:**
   - Data localization requirements (certain categories of data must be stored in Saudi Arabia)
   - Lawful interception capabilities
   - National security review for critical infrastructure
5. **Financial Requirements:**
   - License fees (vary by license type)
   - Revenue-sharing with CST
   - Universal service fund contributions
6. **Saudization Requirements:**
   - Saudi workforce quotas as per Nitaqat program
   - Training and capability building commitments

## Regulatory Framework

### Key Regulations

| Regulation | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Telecom Act** | Primary legislation governing telecom sector |
| **Anti-Competitive Practices Regulations** | Prevents monopolistic behavior |
| **Consumer Protection Regulations** | Service quality, billing, and dispute resolution |
| **Data Protection** | Personal Data Protection Law (PDPL) applies to telecom data |
| **Spectrum Regulations** | Allocation and management of radio frequencies |
| **Interconnection Regulations** | Rules for network interconnection between operators |
| **Number Portability** | Mobile number portability regulations |
| **Infrastructure Sharing** | Tower sharing and duct sharing regulations |

### Spectrum Licensing

- Spectrum auctions conducted by CST
- Major 5G spectrum allocations completed
- mmWave and sub-6GHz bands allocated
- Spectrum fees are a significant component of telecom operating costs

## Recent Changes (2025–2026)

### 5G Expansion
- Saudi Arabia is among the global leaders in 5G deployment
- All three major operators (STC, Mobily, Zain) have extensive 5G networks
- CST continues to allocate additional spectrum for 5G expansion

### CITC to CST Rebrand
- The regulator was renamed from CITC (Communications and Information Technology Commission) to CST (Communications, Space & Technology Commission) to reflect expanded mandate including space technology

### Digital Transformation
- Continued implementation of the National Digital Transformation Strategy
- Expansion of digital services and e-government platforms
- Focus on cloud computing regulation and localization

### Data Protection
- Full enforcement of the Personal Data Protection Law (PDPL)
- Impact on telecom operators' data handling and storage practices

### Open Access & Infrastructure Sharing
- Enhanced regulations for infrastructure sharing
- Fiber optic open access framework being implemented

## Important Notes for Foreign Investors

1. **Dual licensing required** — both MISA investment license AND CST telecom license
2. **Security-sensitive sector** — expect thorough review process
3. **Data localization** — certain data must remain within Saudi Arabia
4. **Market is oligopolistic** — three major operators dominate, but opportunities exist in value-added services, MVNO, and infrastructure
5. **Government is actively encouraging** digital infrastructure investment
6. **PDPL compliance** is mandatory and affects data handling
