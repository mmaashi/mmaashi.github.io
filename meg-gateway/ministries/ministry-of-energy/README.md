## Role & Jurisdiction

The Ministry of Energy oversees all aspects of the Kingdom's energy sector, through an integrated strategy focused on:

### Core Mandate
- **Hydrocarbons** — oil, gas, refined products, and petrochemicals
- **Electricity & Renewable Energy** — thermal, solar, wind, and complementary energy
- **Energy Efficiency** — national energy conservation programs
- **Hydrogen Economy** — blue hydrogen (from hydrocarbons) and green hydrogen (from renewables)
- **Energy Mix Optimization** — most efficient and cost-effective electricity production

### Key Programs
- **Hydrocarbon Demand Sustainability Program** — including plans to make Saudi Arabia the world's largest clean hydrogen exporter
- **Optimal Energy Mix Program** — most efficient, lowest-cost electricity production
- **Energy Efficiency Programs** — conservation and efficiency standards
- **Local Manufacturing Programs** — domestic production of solar and wind energy components

### Related Entities

| Entity | Role |
|--------|------|
| **Saudi Aramco** | State oil company — world's largest |
| **ACWA Power** | Major power and water desalination company |
| **Saudi Electricity Company (SEC)** | Primary electricity provider |
| **WERA** — Water & Electricity Regulatory Authority | Regulates electricity and cogeneration |
| **REPDO** — Renewable Energy Project Development Office | Manages renewable energy procurement |
| **K.A.CARE** — King Abdullah City for Atomic and Renewable Energy | Nuclear energy and research |
| **ENEC** — Energy Efficiency Center | Energy conservation programs |

## Foreign Investment in the Energy Sector

### Overview

The energy sector in Saudi Arabia has historically been the most significant sector for foreign investment, though direct investment in upstream oil & gas has been limited. Under Vision 2030, significant new opportunities are opening across the energy value chain.

### Investment Opportunities by Sub-sector

#### 1. Renewable Energy

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Target** | 50% renewable energy in electricity mix by 2030 |
| **Solar** | Massive solar PV projects under REPDO procurement |
| **Wind** | Onshore wind projects, including Dumat Al Jandal wind farm |
| **Hydrogen** | Green and blue hydrogen — NEOM Green Hydrogen project |
| **Foreign Participation** | Open to foreign investors through BOO/BOOT/IPP models |
| **Key Programs** | National Renewable Energy Program (NREP) |

#### 2. Oil & Gas (Downstream/Services)

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Upstream** | Largely reserved for Saudi Aramco (state monopoly on exploration/production) |
| **Downstream** | Refining, petrochemicals — open to foreign JVs and investment |
| **Services** | Oilfield services, technology, engineering — open to foreign companies |
| **Aramco Partnerships** | SABIC, Motiva, Petro Rabigh — examples of foreign JVs |

#### 3. Electricity & Power Generation

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **IPP Model** | Independent Power Producer model for new capacity |
| **IWPP** | Independent Water & Power Projects |
| **Privatization** | SEC undergoing restructuring and partial privatization |
| **Smart Grid** | Investments in grid modernization and smart meters |

#### 4. Nuclear Energy

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **K.A.CARE** | Manages nuclear energy program |
| **Plans** | Saudi Arabia has plans for civilian nuclear power |
| **Foreign Participation** | Technology partnerships sought with international nuclear companies |

### Requirements for Foreign Energy Companies

1. **MISA Investment License** — mandatory
2. **Ministry of Energy sector authorization** — for specific energy activities
3. **WERA License** — for electricity generation, transmission, or distribution
4. **Environmental permits** — from General Authority for Meteorology and Environmental Protection
5. **Safety certifications** — specific to energy type (nuclear, oil & gas, etc.)
6. **Local content requirements** — In Kingdom Total Value Add (IKTVA) program for Aramco suppliers
7. **Saudization** — Saudi workforce requirements
8. **Joint venture requirements** — some sub-sectors require partnership with Saudi entities

### Renewable Energy Procurement (REPDO)

The Renewable Energy Project Development Office (REPDO) manages the procurement of renewable energy projects:

- **Competitive bidding process** for solar and wind projects
- **Long-term Power Purchase Agreements (PPAs)** — typically 20-25 years
- **BOO model** — Build, Own, Operate
- **Saudi Power Procurement Company (SPPC)** as off-taker
- **Projects range** from hundreds of MW to multi-GW scale
- **Foreign developers welcome** — international companies have won multiple projects

## Recent Changes (2025–2026)

### Renewable Energy Expansion
- Continued aggressive procurement of solar and wind projects
- Several rounds of REPDO auctions completed
- Saudi Arabia achieving some of world's lowest solar energy prices

### Green Hydrogen
- NEOM Green Hydrogen project advancing (partnership with ACWA Power, Air Products)
- Plans to export green hydrogen/ammonia to Asia and Europe
- Hydrogen regulatory framework being developed

### Aramco IPO & Subsequent Developments
- Secondary offering completed in 2024
- IKTVA (In-Kingdom Total Value Add) program requirements increasing
- More opportunities for foreign service companies

### Energy Efficiency
- Saudi Energy Efficiency Center (SEEC) expanding standards
- Building codes, appliance standards, and vehicle efficiency requirements tightening

### Circular Carbon Economy
- Saudi Arabia promoting Circular Carbon Economy framework
- Carbon capture and storage (CCS) projects advancing
- Carbon trading discussions

## Important Notes for Foreign Investors

1. **Upstream oil & gas is NOT open** to direct foreign investment (Aramco monopoly on exploration/production)
2. **Downstream and services are open** — significant opportunities
3. **Renewable energy is the biggest growth area** — competitive bidding through REPDO
4. **IKTVA requirements** — Aramco suppliers must meet increasing local content targets
5. **Long-term PPAs** provide revenue certainty for power projects
6. **Hydrogen economy** is emerging — early movers have advantage
7. **Environmental regulations** are tightening — compliance costs to increase
8. **Energy transition creates opportunities** — traditional energy companies diversifying into renewables
