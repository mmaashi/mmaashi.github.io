## Official Information

Saudi Arabia does not have a single "Ministry of Technology." The technology sector is governed by multiple entities:

### Key Government Bodies

| Entity | Arabic | Role | Website |

| **SDAIA** — Saudi Data & Artificial Intelligence Authority | الهيئة السعودية للبيانات والذكاء الاصطناعي | AI strategy, data governance | https://sdaia.gov.sa |
| **DGA** — Digital Government Authority | هيئة الحكومة الرقمية | E-government, digital services | https://dga.gov.sa |

## Role & Jurisdiction

### MCIT — Ministry of Communications & Information Technology
- Sets national ICT and digital economy policies
- Drives digital transformation across government and private sector
- Oversees technology education and workforce development
- Coordinates international technology partnerships
- **Minister:** Abdullah bin Amer Al-Swaha

### SDAIA — Saudi Data & AI Authority
- Leads the Kingdom's AI strategy
- Manages the National Data Office
- Oversees the Digital Data Marketplace (440+ data-sharing services)
- Manages the National Data Catalog (370+ government systems integrated)
- Implements Personal Data Protection Law (PDPL)
- Vision: "Among the Elite League of Data-Driven Economies"

### DGA — Digital Government Authority
- Regulates government digital platforms
- Issues digital platform licenses (all .gov.sa sites must register)
- Manages national digital identity infrastructure

## Foreign Investment in the Technology Sector

### Overview

Technology and digital are among the **15 priority sectors** for foreign investment under MISA's National Investment Strategy. Saudi Arabia is actively seeking foreign investment in:

- Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning
- Cloud Computing and Data Centers
- Cybersecurity
- FinTech
- EdTech
- HealthTech
- IoT and Smart City technologies
- Space technology
- Semiconductor and chip design
- Software development and SaaS

### Investment Requirements

1. **MISA Investment License** — standard requirement for all foreign investors
2. **Sector-specific compliance:**
   - **AI/Data:** Compliance with SDAIA regulations and PDPL
   - **Cloud Computing:** CST cloud computing framework compliance, data localization requirements
   - **FinTech:** Additional licensing from Saudi Central Bank (SAMA) or Capital Market Authority (CMA)
   - **HealthTech:** Additional licensing from Ministry of Health or Saudi FDA
   - **Cybersecurity:** National Cybersecurity Authority (NCA) compliance

### 100% Foreign Ownership

- Technology companies **can be 100% foreign-owned** in most sub-sectors
- Some exceptions for defense-related or national security technology
- The new Investment Law (2024/2025) has further liberalized ownership rules

### Data Localization & Privacy

| Requirement | Details |
|------------|---------|
| **Personal Data Protection Law (PDPL)** | Comprehensive data protection regulation, enforced by SDAIA |
| **Data Localization** | Certain categories of sensitive data must be stored within Saudi Arabia |
| **Cross-border Data Transfer** | Permitted under specific conditions with adequate safeguards |
| **Data Classification** | Government data classified into sensitivity levels |

## Important Notes for Foreign Investors

1. **No single ministry governs technology** — navigate multiple regulators depending on sub-sector
2. **Data localization is serious** — plan cloud architecture accordingly
3. **PDPL compliance is mandatory** — significant penalties for violations
4. **Government is a major customer** — Saudi government's digital transformation creates large procurement opportunities
5. **Talent competition** — Saudization requirements combined with high demand for tech talent creates unique staffing challenges
6. **Vision 2030 alignment** — technology investments that align with Vision 2030 goals receive favorable treatment
7. **NEOM and mega-projects** — create massive technology demand and investment opportunities
