## Real Estate / Renting (العقارات)

### Key Platforms

| Platform | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| **Property Finder** | Largest property portal |
| **Bayut** | Strong Dubai presence |
| **Dubizzle** | Classifieds (apartments, rooms) |
| **Google Maps** | Find agents in areas |

### Rental Process

1. **Search** — Online or through agent
2. **View** — Schedule viewing
3. **Negotiate** — Often negotiable (5-10%)
4. **Hold Deposit** — Typically 2-5% to hold
5. **Contract** — 1-year contract (12 post-dated cheques)
6. **Ejari** — Online registration (required)
7. **DEWA** — Connect utilities

### Key Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Cheque** | Post-dated payments (standard) |
| **Ejari** | Rental contract registration (mandatory) |
| **DEWA** | Dubai Electricity & Water Authority |
| **ADDC** | Abu Dhabi Distribution Company |
| **Agent Fee** | Typically 5% of annual rent |
| **Security Deposit** | Usually 5-10% of annual rent |

- **Duration:** 10 years (renewable)

- **License:** Exchangeable from 50+ countries, otherwise test required
- **Side:** Left-hand drive (like UK)
- **Petrol:** Cheap (~AED 2-3/liter)

- **Monthly:** AED 2,000-6,000

- **Requirements:** Passport, driving license, credit card

## Lifestyle & Culture

### What to Expect

- **Liberal (for GCC):** More relaxed than Saudi
- **International:** 80%+ expat population
- **Weekend:** Friday-Saturday
- **Alcohol:** Available in licensed venues and at home (for non-Muslims)
- **Dress Code:** Modest but less strict than Saudi
- **Ramadan:** Business hours adjusted but less disruptive

### Pros

- ✅ Tax-free income (no income tax)
- ✅ World-class infrastructure
- ✅ Safe and clean
- ✅ International lifestyle
- ✅ Easy travel hub (DXB/AUH airports)
- ✅ Multiple choices in everything (schools, hospitals, restaurants)

### Challenges

- ⚠️ High cost of living (especially rent)
- ⚠️ Can feel transient
- ⚠️ Hot climate (40°C+ summer)
- ⚠️ Visa tied to employment
- ⚠️ Limited integration with locals
